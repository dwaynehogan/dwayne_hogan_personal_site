Draft Ugly

Why Bad Work Is The Key To Being A Prolific Creator

You've been looking for the thing that's stopping you. The block. The fear. The Resistance.

You've read the books. You've bought the tools. You've taken the courses. You've tried every framework, every system, every hack. And none of it worked — because all of it was built on a lie.

The lie is that Resistance is something outside you. A dark force. An ancient enemy. Something you fight, push through, overcome.

But if Resistance is external, why does it know exactly where you're weak? Why does it follow you from project to project, year to year, tool to tool?

Because it's not outside you.

You are Resistance.

Not because you're broken. Not because you're lazy. But because identity is built through action — and you've spent years taking the wrong ones. Every course instead of drafting. Every plan instead of starting. Every "tomorrow" instead of today. You weren't preparing. You were becoming.

And what you became is the thing that's stopping you.

You've been practicing the Religion of Ready. Waiting to be saved. Waiting for clarity. Waiting until it's good enough. But readiness is Resistance wearing a responsible mask. And consumption is its sacrament — every book, every course, every tool is communion at the wrong altar.

The way out is heresy.

Draft something bad. Draft it today. That's the blasphemy that sets you free.

A bad draft isn't a step toward a good draft. It's more valuable than a good draft — because it's proof you're becoming someone else.

Every draft, no matter how ugly, is a vote for the creator. Every blank canvas is a vote for Resistance. You've been voting the same way for years. And you're winning the wrong election.

No one's coming to save you.

No framework. No book. No mentor. No moment of clarity.

Just you. And what you draft. Or don't.

You can stay Resistance forever. That's also a choice. You can die as the enemy. No one will stop you.

Or you can draft ugly. Today. And start becoming someone new.
