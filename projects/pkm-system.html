Personal Knowledge Management System

Overview

A comprehensive system for capturing, organizing, and leveraging knowledge to enhance creativity, productivity, and continuous learning.

The Problem

In our information-rich world, the challenge isn't finding information—it's:

  • Capturing insights before they're forgotten
  • Organizing knowledge so it's findable and useful
  • Connecting ideas across different domains
  • Applying learning to real-world projects

Without a system, valuable insights get lost, learning doesn't compound, and we constantly rediscover things we already knew.

My Approach

I've built a knowledge management system based on these principles:

1. Capture Everything

No idea is too small to capture. I use:

  • Quick Capture - Notion mobile app for immediate thoughts
  • Reading Notes - Highlights and reflections from articles and books
  • Meeting Notes - Key takeaways and action items
  • Project Logs - What I learned during each project

2. Process Regularly

Weekly review sessions to:

  • Process captured notes into permanent knowledge
  • Create connections between related ideas
  • Archive or delete what's no longer relevant
  • Surface insights ready to be applied

3. Organize for Discovery

My system is organized around:

  • Topics - Core areas of interest (Marketing, Creativity, Systems Thinking)
  • Projects - Active work with linked knowledge
  • Resources - Curated collections of valuable content
  • Evergreen Notes - Permanent, atomic ideas that build over time

4. Connect Ideas

The real value comes from connections:

  • Bidirectional linking between related concepts
  • Emergence of patterns across different domains
  • Unexpected insights from cross-domain thinking

Key Features

Evergreen Notes System

Inspired by Andy Matuschak's approach:

  • One idea per note
  • Written in my own words
  • Densely linked to related concepts
  • Continuously refined over time

Project-Knowledge Integration

Every project:

  • Links to relevant knowledge notes
  • Creates new knowledge as I learn
  • Feeds back into the system
  • Builds expertise over time

Reading Workflow

A systematic approach to learning from reading:

  1. Capture highlights while reading
  2. Review highlights after finishing
  3. Extract key insights into permanent notes
  4. Connect to existing knowledge
  5. Apply to active projects

Tools & Technology

  • Notion - Primary system for organization and linking
  • Readwise - Highlight capture from books and articles
  • Voice memos - Quick capture when mobile
  • Regular reviews - Weekly processing and connection-making

Impact

This system has:

  • Reduced anxiety about forgetting important insights
  • Accelerated learning by building on previous knowledge
  • Enhanced creativity through unexpected connections
  • Improved writing by having a repository of developed thoughts

Principles I Follow

  1. Lower the friction for capture - Make it effortless to save ideas
  2. Process regularly - Don't let the inbox overflow
  3. Write for your future self - Make notes clear and useful
  4. Focus on connections - Links are where the magic happens
  5. Let the system evolve - Structure should serve you, not constrain you

Challenges & Solutions

Information Overload

  • Solution: Be selective about what enters the system
  • Focus on insights, not just information
  • Regular pruning of what's no longer relevant

Time Investment

  • Solution: Make it part of existing workflows
  • Capture during activities you already do
  • Process notes during existing review time

Over-Organization

  • Solution: Start simple, add structure as needed
  • Optimize for retrieval, not just organization
  • Let organic structure emerge

What's Next

Continuing to refine:

  • AI assistance for note processing and connection-making
  • Better integration with project workflows
  • More sophisticated linking and emergence patterns
  • Publishing evergreen notes publicly
